The Details Are Not the Details

Why attention to detail matters

Why this matters professionally

  1. Portfolio reviewers notice inconsistent spacing in seconds
  2. Clients equate visual sloppiness with unreliability
  3. Details signal: "I care about my work and I'll care about yours"

Why we miss details

  1. Familiarity blindness — you've looked at your design so many times
  2. "I'll fix it later" syndrome — but later never comes
  3. Not knowing what to look for — you can't see what you don't know to check

The mindset shift

  1. Don't treat polish as a final step — build it into how you work
  2. Slow down: zoom to 100%, check every element
  3. Fresh eyes help: take a break, or have someone else look

This isn't about perfection — it's about intention. Every pixel should be deliberate.

Attention to detail is a habit to develop, not a step to add at the end.

Detail Audit

Systematic review activity

Alignment

  • All text left edges align to the same grid lines
  • Images and cards snap to grid
  • No elements that are "close but not quite" aligned

Check now: Turn on your grid in Figma. Is everything snapping?

Spacing

  • Consistent padding inside all containers
  • Consistent margins between sections
  • Related items grouped tightly; unrelated items have clear separation

Remember Gestalt: proximity creates relationships.

Typography

  • No more than 2-3 type sizes used intentionally
  • Line lengths between 45-75 characters
  • No orphans (single words on a line) or widows
  • All text is actual text, not placeholder

Responsive

  • Mobile text is readable (16px+ body)
  • Touch targets are 44px+ minimum
  • Nothing cut off or awkwardly wrapped

Consistency

  • Button styles match throughout
  • Icon styles are consistent (outline OR filled, not mixed)
  • Colors match your defined palette exactly

Mark issues as you find them. This is your fix-it list.
