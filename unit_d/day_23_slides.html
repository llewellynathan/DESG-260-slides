Storytelling & the Case Study

Not a lab report — a narrative

Every great story has the same arc

  1. Situation — Who has a problem? Why does it matter?
  2. Tension — What did you discover? What was hard? What didn't work?
  3. Resolution — What did you build? How does it solve the problem?

The key question for every section: "What did I learn?" — not "What did I make?"

Your case study sections tell a three-act story

Act 1 — Setup

Title page, creative brief, persona, research, journey map — Who is this for and why should we care?

Act 2 — Conflict

Competitive analysis, wireframes, usability testing — What problems did you discover? How did your design evolve?

Act 3 — Resolution

Style guide, high-fidelity screens, motion design — The polished solution, informed by everything before

Epilogue

AI usage documentation — Honest reflection on your process and tools

Show the messy middle.

Iterations, dead ends, and pivots show how you think. Perfection doesn't.

What makes a compelling case study

Before/after comparisons with annotations explaining why you changed it

"I tested it and discovered X, so I changed Y"

Common mistakes: screenshot dump with no thread  ·  skipping to the final screens  ·  hiding the failures

Think about your testing debrief stories from earlier — those surprises and pivots are exactly what belongs in your case study.

Write your project's story in one paragraph

  1. Sentence 1: Who has the problem and what is it?
  2. Sentence 2: What did you discover through research and testing?
  3. Sentence 3: What did you build and why does it work?

Share with your partner. Does it make sense to someone who's never seen your screens? This paragraph becomes the opening of your case study.

Your case study is a design artifact

Apply the same craft principles from Day 21 to the document itself:

  1. Title — Large, bold. Project name and one-line description.
  2. Section heads — Clear, consistent. Guide the reader through the narrative.
  3. Body text — Readable, well-spaced. The bulk of your story.
  4. Captions — Small, paired with images. Explain what the reader is seeing.

Consistent layout: margins, columns, image placement, whitespace. No "Lorem ipsum."
