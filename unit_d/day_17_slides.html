Paper Prototyping

Why sketch before you commit to pixels

Speed enables iteration

  1. Paper is fast — you can sketch 10 screens in the time it takes to make 1 in Figma
  2. Paper invites feedback — people critique pixels, but they help improve sketches
  3. Paper prevents attachment — you'll iterate more if you haven't invested hours

The goal isn't beautiful sketches — it's fast learning.

What makes a prototype "testable"?

To Scale

Sketch at actual phone size — index cards work great (3x5")

Realistic Content

Real text, not "lorem ipsum" — fake content hides problems

Detailed Enough to Use

Someone should be able to "tap" through your prototype

Clear Tap Targets

Where would someone actually touch?
